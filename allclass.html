DAkhil Section

Every year students from different place are admitted to our madrasa. Our student population at the entry level is approximatly 150. Dakhil Madrasa (Arabic: مدرسة الداخل‎) is a level of Madrasa where education is provided from class one to class ten and at the end of class ten a Dakhil pass certificate is awarded through a public examination which is equivalent to the Secondary School Certificate (S.S.C.). This education system was originally started with the aim of providing religious education to Muslim children along with general education. That is, it has been created by combining general education (school-college) and religious education (Qawmi Madrasa) parts into an education system/syllabus. And Dakhil Madrasas are run by the government and financed by the government.

Curriculum Edit Dakhil Madrasahs provide education at various levels and in various departments. For example: Dakhil 6th-8th Dakhil General Dakhil Mujabbid Dakhil Science Dakhil Hifzul Quran Dakhil Business Studies Recently, a decision has been taken to grant permission to teach the subject of 'Information and Communication Technology' in Dakhil Madrasahs. Examination System: The procedure for the Dakhil examination is the same as that of the SSC examination. Each subject has a 3-hour examination, the full value of which is 100 marks. The examination contains both 'multiple choice' and 'creative' types of questions. There is no break between the 'multiple choice' and 'creative' tests. If the total value of the 'multiple choice' questions is 30 marks, the allotted time is 30 minutes. On the other hand, if the total value of the 'multiple choice' questions is 25 marks, the time allotted is 30 minutes.

To run a Dakhil Madrasa, official recognition from the Bangladesh Madrasa Education Board is required. The Madrasa Education Act of 1978 established this board to regulate the Madrasa education system in Bangladesh. Entrance examinations are conducted by this board and admission letters are issued by the board based on the results of the students. Departments such as science, commerce, etc. can be opened with the permission of this council. Officials of the board regularly visit Dakhil Madrasas and evaluate the educational activities.
